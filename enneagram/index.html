What is the Enneagram?

The Enneagram is a personality system that describes nine distinct personality types, each with its own motivations, fears, and growth paths. Unlike other personality assessments, the Enneagram focuses on why you behave the way you do, rather than just how you behave.

Each of the nine types has a core motivation and fear that drives their behavior. Understanding your Enneagram type can provide deep insights into your patterns, relationships, and personal development journey.

The 9 Enneagram Types

Each Enneagram type has unique characteristics, motivations, and growth opportunities. Click on any type below to learn more.

Type 1 - The Perfectionist

Rational, principled, and self-controlled. Type 1s strive for perfection and improvement.

Type 2 - The Helper

Caring, generous, and people-pleasing. Type 2s want to be loved and needed.

Type 3 - The Achiever

Success-oriented, adaptable, and image-conscious. Type 3s want to be valuable and worthwhile.

Type 4 - The Individualist

Expressive, dramatic, and self-absorbed. Type 4s want to be unique and find their identity.

Type 5 - The Investigator

Intense, cerebral, and perceptive. Type 5s want to be capable and competent.

Type 6 - The Loyalist

Engaging, responsible, and anxious. Type 6s want to have security and support.

Type 7 - The Enthusiast

Spontaneous, versatile, and scattered. Type 7s want to maintain happiness and satisfaction.

Type 8 - The Challenger

Self-confident, decisive, and confrontational. Type 8s want to be self-reliant and in control.

Type 9 - The Peacemaker

Receptive, reassuring, and complacent. Type 9s want to create harmony and avoid conflict.

  • Growth Paths: Discover specific paths for personal development and integration
  • Relationship Insights: Understand how your type interacts with others
